First Term Reflection

December 24 2020

As my first term of the Computing and Financial Management program at the University of Waterloo came to an end, I’ve taken some time to look back at the last 4 months and think about important things I’ve learned.

1. You are capable of doing so much more than you think you can.

THIS has got to be one of the biggest lessons I’ve learned and internalized over the last 4 months. During the first semester, I was faced with many academic challenges that I didn’t believe I could overcome. I felt especially hopeless when it came to solving math problems from our weekly assignments that looked absolutely impossible. But there was no other option other than to do everything I could to solve as much of the problem as possible.

On Mondays when assignments were released, I would read over the questions and feel dread. Yet as I worked on the questions bit by bit each day, I would slowly make progress and feel my excitement grow as I neared a solution. Most of the time, a problem that initially seemed impossible would become solvable.

By the last few weeks of the first term, I felt less dread and uncertainty when tackling difficult problems. I had increased confidence in myself and knew that even if a question seemed impossible at first, all I had to do was calm my mind down and try to take the first small step.

2. Create structure in an unstructured world.

Due to having asynchronous online classes, my default schedule was very unstructured and I relied heavily on self-discipline to maintain structure in my day-to-day life. As much as I enjoy spontaneity and ‘going with the flow’, I personally find structure to be an important backbone in maintaining my life. In the first half of the term, my sleep schedule had completely fallen apart, sleeping at 3 or 4am most nights. This was a big change given that I slept religiously at 11pm as a high schooler. To restore some order back into my life, I set a hard rule to sleep before 2am and I preplanned my weeks by allocating my weekly tasks in a spread out manner. To be honest, I only followed my rule and preplanned my weeks about 60% of the time, but I am working on improving my success rate in the next semester.

3. Keeping in touch with family is important.

It’s very easy to get caught up in your own world when experiencing a completely new environment such as university. Part of what keeps me grounded is feeling grateful for my family that supported me throughout my journey to get to where I am today. Calling them on a weekly basis to update them about university life was one of my personal goals.

The pandemic caused the world to slow down and made me appreciate my family much more for everything they’ve done for me. I also came across an eye-opening article written by Tim Urban about how you spend the majority of the time you’ll ever get with your parents by the time you’re 18. As a result, I’ve been trying to be more intentional with family time after moving out.

Those were 3 out of many lessons that I’ve learned over my first 4 months in university. Although it has been challenging, the fun comes with overcoming the struggle.
